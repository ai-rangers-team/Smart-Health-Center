Smart Health Centre

A real-time command centre for district health officials — and a 60-second daily report app for the PHC operator. Live on Google Cloud, in English, Hindi and Marathi.

Smart Health Centre01
The Problem

A district runs 30+ health centres on paper registers and phone calls

Primary Health Centres are rural India's front door to healthcare. But the people responsible for them are flying blind.

Stock-outs surface too late

A medicine shortage is discovered when a patient is turned away — not two weeks earlier, when a transfer or reorder could still have prevented it.

No live operational picture

Footfall, bed availability, staff attendance and diagnostics reach the district as monthly paperwork. Underperforming centres hide in the averages.

Tools aren't built for the users

PHC staff work in Marathi and Hindi on low-end phones. Dashboards built for English-speaking analysts never get adopted at the last mile.

One centre's surplus is another centre's stock-out. The data to fix this exists — it just arrives a month late, on paper.
Smart Health Centre02
The Solution

Report once. The district sees everything, instantly.

  • PHC operator app — the whole daily report (patients, staff, stock, beds, tests) takes under a minute on a phone, in the operator's own language.
  • District command centre — live status of every centre: AI morning briefing, stock-out forecasts, alerts, performance flags, transfer recommendations.
  • Real-time, not batch — every save recomputes forecasts, scores and alerts, and the dashboard updates in about a second. No refresh, no report cycle.

Built around one principle

Algorithms decide. Gemini explains.
Every alert and forecast is deterministic and auditable. Generative AI is the communication layer — briefings, explanations, document reading — never the gatekeeper of a health decision.

Smart Health Centre06
AI Architecture

Algorithms decide. Gemini explains.

Health alerts must be reproducible and auditable. So the decision path is pure deterministic code — and Gemini makes it human.

Deterministic core — the decision path

  • Recency-weighted consumption forecasting (normalized EWMA) predicts the stock-out date of every medicine at every centre.
  • Composite performance score across attendance, footfall, stock and diagnostics flags underperforming centres automatically.
  • Redistribution engine matches surplus centres to deficit centres before stock-outs happen. Template-based alerts, zero hallucination risk.
  • New centres are safe from day one: stock thresholds derived from one admin-friendly question — expected patients per day.

Gemini 2.5 Flash — the communication layer

  • Morning district briefing written from live data, in the official's language, with cached last-good fallback so it never goes blank.
  • Plain-language explanations of why a centre is underperforming and why a transfer is recommended.
  • Invoice OCR: photo or PDF to structured stock quantities, constrained to the centre's own catalog.
  • Never on the live write path — if Gemini is down or rate-limited, every alert, forecast and save still works.

Smart Health Centre09
Impact & Scale

Built for one district. Ready for a state.

Onboarding is one question

A new PHC gets safe stock thresholds from "roughly how many patients a day?" — and an entire district onboards from a single CSV: 30–40 centres, operators provisioned automatically.

The model rolls up

Centre to district today; district to state next. The same scoring, forecasting and redistribution logic works at every level of aggregation.

Integration roadmap

Designed to plug into e-Aushadhi (drug logistics), HMIS reporting and ABDM registries — replacing parallel data entry, not adding to it.

Every day of earlier warning is a queue of patients who did not get turned away. The marginal cost of adding a centre is one row in a CSV.
